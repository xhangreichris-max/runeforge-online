Goblin Name Generator — D&D, WoW & Fantasy Names

Goblins are one of the most beloved creatures in fantasy because they occupy a unique space between comic and dangerous. In Dungeons & Dragons they are cunning, chaotic dungeon-dwellers who serve as everything from lowly minions to surprisingly clever antagonists. In World of Warcraft they are fast-talking engineers and traders driven by explosive ambition. In both settings and across countless other fantasy worlds, a goblin's name says everything about who they are — and names that fit their frantic energy make encounters, characters, and campaigns more memorable.

This goblin name generator was built for dungeon masters, players, writers, and worldbuilders who need names that feel authentically goblin — short, punchy, slightly chaotic, and impossible to forget. The generator covers five distinct styles so you can match the tone of your specific setting.

What Makes a Great Goblin Name?

The best goblin names share a few key qualities. They are almost always short — one or two syllables at most. They favor hard consonants like g, k, x, z, and r that feel aggressive and sharp. They often have a comedic edge while still sounding genuinely menacing. Names like Snik, Krax, Zibbit, and Grub work because they are easy to say quickly, they sound vaguely threatening, and they have a chaotic rhythm that matches goblin personalities.

Goblin names also frequently carry function. Many goblins earn descriptive names based on their behavior, specialty, or a notable incident. A goblin who accidentally blew up three catapults might be called Boompaw or Sparktooth. This nickname tradition is especially strong in WoW goblins, where earned titles and reputations become part of the identity.

Goblin Naming Conventions by Setting

D&D goblins follow naming conventions established in official sourcebooks. These names are guttural, short, and functional — rarely exceeding two syllables, avoiding any sense of elegance or nobility.

WoW goblins from the Bilgewater Cartel have a more energetic naming culture. Their names still favor short, punchy sounds but often include a sharper edge of personality — names that suggest greed, speed, and ambition.

Male goblin names tend toward aggressive sounds — hard stops, growling consonants, and clipped endings. Female goblin names carry the same energy but sometimes include slightly softer vowels, though never soft enough to suggest anything other than a goblin who has outsmarted everyone in the room.

How to Use Goblin Names in Your Campaign

Naming Goblin NPCs Quickly

Dungeon masters often need goblin names on the fly — a party ambushes a goblin camp and suddenly needs six distinct individuals. The key to fast goblin naming is keeping a mental vocabulary of sounds: sn, gr, kr, zz, ix, ub, rot, nub, ax. Combine two and you have a goblin name. This generator does exactly that systematically, so you can generate a batch before your session.

Goblin Bosses and Named Villains

When a goblin becomes a recurring antagonist, their name needs to carry more weight. Names like Kraknub, Snikrot, and Zaxrix work because they are memorable enough to stick while still sounding definitively goblin. Pairing a strong name with a distinctive title — Kraknub the Unburned, Snikrot of the Bleeding Eye — transforms a random encounter into a character.

Goblin Names for Player Characters

Goblin player characters have become increasingly popular in D&D 5e since the race became officially playable. A goblin PC name that balances comic and capable can set the tone for the entire playthrough. Many players enjoy names that hint at a backstory — a goblin named Twitchpaw might have earned the name through nervous energy or a forgotten injury.

Goblins Across Fantasy Worlds

Goblins appear in virtually every fantasy tradition. In modern tabletop and video game design, goblins have evolved into one of the most culturally varied fantasy races — capable of being anything from terrifying raiders to lovable chaos agents. This diversity means goblin names can cover a wide tonal range while still feeling authentic to the setting.
