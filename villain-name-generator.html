What Makes a Good Villain Name?

The most memorable villain names share qualities that make them stick in a reader's or player's mind long after the story ends.

  • Hard phonetics. Consonants like K, X, Z, and V — and fricatives like Sh, Kr, Thr — create a sense of danger. Soft names can work for elegant villains but typically need a darker title to balance them.
  • Meaning and connotation. Names that evoke darkness, destruction, void, blood, or shadow carry subconscious weight. "Mournfall" tells you about sorrow and collapse before you read a word of lore.
  • A title or epithet. "Zareth" is a name. "Zareth Mournfall, Harbinger of Ruin" is a villain. A good title communicates power level, domain, and personality instantly.
  • Pronounceability. Names that can be said aloud without stumbling stick better in memory. Use apostrophes sparingly — one per name maximum.
  • Setting fit. Dark fantasy villains need archaic, guttural sounds. Sci-fi antagonists work better with sharp, mechanical, or corporate-sounding names like "Commander Null" or "Archon Xelvar."
